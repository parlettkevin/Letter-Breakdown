# Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
**Primary**: Loan officers at DML (a mortgage lender) — Kevin Parlett and Corey Glowacki are named signers — using this tool on their phones during off-hours. The core scenario: sitting at a son's lacrosse game on a Saturday when a borrower calls needing numbers and a pre-approval letter. The alternative is going to the car, finding a laptop, and making the borrower wait. This tool eliminates that.

**Job to be done**: Stay on the phone, enter a handful of numbers, get a payment figure to say out loud, then email a PDF letter to the borrower before hanging up. The borrower never sees the screen — the loan officer is reading numbers aloud and emailing a document.

**What this is NOT**: An open-house tool where a borrower glances at the screen. The audience for the visual output is the loan officer only, reading while half-listening on a call.

### Deliverables — Both Are First Class
There are two outputs, and both matter equally:
1. **Pre-approval PDF** — downloaded and emailed/shared from the phone via Web Share API. Layout and branding are locked. Design work only touches the interactive UI that drives it, not the letter itself.
2. **Copy-to-SMS / iMessage** — a plain-text loan summary the officer pastes into a text message. This is a primary workflow, not a fallback. It should feel as intentional as the PDF button.

### Brand Personality
**Three words**: Swift, grounded, trusted.

- **Swift**: The tool exists because laptops aren't always handy. Every friction point costs a loan officer credibility in front of a borrower.
- **Grounded**: Warm neutrals, not cold blues or flashy gradients. Serious money; the tone should match.
- **Trusted**: Competent and authoritative without being intimidating. Borrowers may glance at the screen — it should look like a real professional tool, not a web form.

**Existing brand color**: Orange — `#f4791f` (DML's primary CTA color, focus rings, primary buttons). This is locked.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Theme**: Light. Variable lighting conditions — outdoors at sporting events, offices, parked cars. Light is safer and more readable in bright environments.

**Visual tone**: Refined utility. A precision field instrument — well-made, slightly warm, clean enough to scan at a glance mid-conversation, dense enough to fit meaningful data without excessive scrolling.

**Anti-references**:
- NOT real estate listing sites (busy, image-heavy, card-grid aesthetic)
- NOT generic bank portals (cold, corporate, forms-heavy with no personality)
- NOT trendy fintech (dark gradients, neon, style over data density)

**References to aim for**: The feel of a well-designed field tool — a surveyor's app, a clinical intake form at a high-end medical practice, a premium handheld calculator. Functional, legible, slightly warmer than clinical.

### Hard Constraints — Do Not Touch
- **DML logos**: All logo assets are locked. Do not resize, recolor, reposition, or replace them.
- **Pre-approval letter layout**: The PDF output (letter format, content, branding) is locked. Design work only affects the interactive calculator UI, never the letter's printable/PDF presentation.

**Color palette — three-tier amber family extending from brand orange:**

### Current UI Surface — What's in Scope
Single `index.html` at repo root. Key functional surfaces as of June 2026:

**Input section (pre-Calculate):**
- Purchase price: `type=text` with live comma formatting and `$` prefix overlay
- Down payment, rate, term, loan type (FHA/Conventional/VA/USDA/Jumbo) with loan-specific fields
- Recurring costs card: property tax (with county estimator), hazard, HOA, closing month/day
- Closing costs card: lender & appraisal fees, lender's/owner's title insurance, seller credit, FTHB checkbox, county transfer split %, recordation split % (default 50), govt recording fees $ (default $125)
- Escrow cushion cap (collapsible `<details>`)
- Sticky Calculate bar with iOS safe area inset

**Results section (post-Calculate):**
- Loan overview card
- Monthly payment card (P&I, hazard, taxes, MIP/PMI, HOA → Total)
- Cash to close card (closing costs, title & settlement breakdown with 7 sub-items indented, transfer taxes, prepaids/reserves with escrow detail → Total)
- Download options card: radio (layout 1 vs 2), signer dropdown, Letter dates collapsible `<details>`, Copy Text button, Download PDF button, `#pdf-output` div (auto-scrolls into view after generation)

**Supporting systems:**
- Signer dropdown: Kevin Parlett / Corey Glowacki — controls PDF signature block
- Session persistence via `localStorage` — prevents iOS Safari back-button data loss
- ZIP input: auto-fills city placeholder hint
- County selector: auto-populates transfer/recordation rates and tax estimator
- `prefers-reduced-motion` respected on all animations

**Animations applied:**
- Result cards stagger in on Calculate (`resultCardIn`, 350ms, 60ms delays)
- Details expansion (`detailsIn`, 200ms)
- Copy Text success state transition
- Warnings fade in

### Design Principles
1. **Mobile speed first** — 480px max-width, designed for use while on a phone call. All inputs 16px+ to prevent iOS zoom. 44px minimum tap targets. No horizontal scrolling ever.
2. **Hierarchy through restraint** — orange accent is rare and intentional. One primary action per screen. Section labels muted amber, totals in deep amber, buttons orange. Three tiers, no more.
3. **Scan before you read** — the loan officer is half-listening on a call. Monthly payment and cash-to-close totals must pop at a glance. Not giant — clearly heavier and warmer than surrounding rows.
4. **Professional, not sterile** — warm neutral tones throughout. The amber family gives it personality without looking promotional. It should feel like a tool a real professional built for themselves.
5. **Two outputs, one flow** — PDF email and SMS copy are co-equal deliverables. Both live in the same Download options card. PDF uses Web Share API for direct phone-to-email delivery.
6. **Reduce scroll before Calculate** — secondary workflow items (letter dates, escrow cushion cap) are collapsed. Government fee adjustments (transfer split, recordation split, recording fees) are candidates for further collapse.

### Open Design Opportunities (prioritized)
1. **Monthly payment card prominence** — it's the key number but looks like every other card. A subtle warm background tint would signal "this is the answer."
2. **PDF generation loading state** — "Download preapproval letter (PDF)" button has no feedback during jsPDF processing. Should disable and show "Generating…" to prevent double-taps.
3. **Copy Text visual parity** — currently secondary (gray) vs PDF primary (orange). `.impeccable.md` principle: both are co-equal. Consider equal visual weight for both delivery buttons.
4. **Collapse government fee fields** — county transfer split, recordation split, recording fees rarely change. Candidates for a `<details>` to reduce pre-calculate scroll.
5. **Warning icon** — amber warning box is good but easy to scroll past. A `⚠` prefix per warning line aids rapid scanning mid-call.
6. **Sticky bar fade** — hard `border-top` edge on the sticky Calculate bar. A gradient fade from transparent to `#fff` above it would feel more native.
7. **Cash to close sub-item grouping** — 7 indented title & settlement rows could use a subtle background tint to visually cluster them.
